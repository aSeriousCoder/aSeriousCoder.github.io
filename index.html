Publications

  • [M2] Simulating News Recommendation Ecosystem for Fun and Profit. (Under Review).

    Guangping Zhang, Dongsheng Li, Hansu Gu, Tun Lu, Li Shang, Ning Gu.

Understanding the evolution of online news communities is essential for designing more effective news recommender systems. However, due to the lack of appropriate datasets and platforms, the existing literature is limited in understanding the impact of recommender systems on this evolutionary process and the underlying mechanisms, resulting in sub-optimal system designs that may affect long-term utilities. In this work, we propose SimuLine, a simulation platform to dissect the evolution of news recommendation ecosystems and present a detailed analysis of the evolutionary process and underlying mechanisms. SimuLine first constructs a latent space well reflecting the human behaviors, and then simulates the news recommendation ecosystem via agent-based modeling. Based on extensive simulation experiments and the comprehensive analysis framework consisting of quantitative metrics, visualization, and textual explanations, we analyze the characteristics of each evolutionary phase from the perspective of life-cycle theory, and propose a relationship graph illustrating the key factors and affecting mechanisms. Furthermore, we explore the impacts of recommender system designing strategies, including the utilization of cold-start news, breaking news, and promotion, on the evolutionary process, which shed new light on the design of recommender systems.

  • [M1] Heterogeneous Graph Neural Network with Personalized and Adaptive Diversity for News Recommendation. (Under Review).

    Guangping Zhang, Dongsheng Li, Hansu Gu, Tun Lu, Ning Gu.

The emerging online media have facilitated the dissemination of news, but have also brought the information overload problem. Therefore, personalized news recommendation has become increasingly important recently. However, existing approaches lack effective utilization of the heterogeneous news content and heterogeneous user-news relationships, resulting in inferior recommendation accuracy. Moreover, prior works define recommendation diversity as the average in-list diversity of all the users at all times, ignoring the personalized and adaptive demands of users for news diversity. In this paper, we first build an attributed heterogeneous graph to organize the heterogeneous information (nodes, relations, and attributes) in the news recommendation. Then, we propose a heterogeneous node content adapter to deal with the semantic gaps and integrate the multiple node attributes. We utilize a variational attributed heterogeneous graph neural network with relation pruning to identify important relations and learn the distributions of user and news representations from both the heterogeneous contents and relationships. The variational distributions learn the interest ranges of the users and the audience ranges of the news, supporting personalized diversity in news recommendations. Finally, we devise a time-continuous exponentially decaying distribution cache to model the temporal dynamics of user real-time interests for providing adaptive news diversity. Through a functional and modular architecture, the proposed method could update efficiently and deal with the cold-start news promptly. Extensive experiments on real-world news datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
